The Lion and the Mouse

Once upon a time, a mighty lion was sleeping in the forest. A tiny mouse ran over his nose and woke him up. The lion was angry and caught the mouse.

"Please let me go," said the mouse. "One day I will help you." The lion laughed and let the mouse go.

Later, the lion was caught in a hunter's net. The mouse heard his roar and came to help. The mouse chewed through the net and freed the lion.

Moral: Even small friends can be great friends.

The Ant and the Grasshopper

On a beautiful summer day, a grasshopper was singing and dancing. He saw an ant working hard, carrying food to her home.

"Why are you working so hard?" asked the grasshopper. "Come and play with me!"

"I'm storing food for winter," said the ant. "You should do the same."

The grasshopper laughed and continued playing. When winter came, the grasshopper had no food and was very hungry, while the ant had plenty to eat.

Moral: Work hard today to enjoy tomorrow.

The Thirsty Crow

A thirsty crow found a pot with very little water. The water was too low for the crow to reach. The crow thought of a plan.

He started dropping pebbles into the pot. As he dropped more pebbles, the water level rose. Soon, the water was high enough for the crow to drink.

Moral: Where there is a will, there is a way.

The Tortoise and the Hare

A hare made fun of a tortoise for being slow. The tortoise challenged the hare to a race. The hare ran fast and soon was far ahead.

The hare decided to take a nap, thinking he would win easily. Meanwhile, the tortoise kept moving slowly but steadily.

When the hare woke up, he saw the tortoise had already won the race.

Moral: Slow and steady wins the race.

The Shepherd Boy and the Wolf

A shepherd boy used to play a trick on the villagers by shouting "Wolf! Wolf!" when there was no wolf. The villagers would come running, only to find no wolf.

One day, a real wolf came. The boy shouted for help, but no one believed him. The wolf attacked the sheep.

Moral: No one believes a liar, even when they tell the truth.
