2. The Boy Who Cried Wolf

A young shepherd boy grew bored while watching his sheep. To amuse himself, he cried, "Wolf! Wolf!" even though no wolf was near. The villagers came running to help, but the boy laughed and admitted it was a joke. He did this again, and once more, the villagers came running. But when a real wolf actually appeared, and the boy shouted for help, no one came because they thought he was lying again. The wolf ate the sheep.

What is the moral of the story?

3. The Fox and the Grapes

A hungry fox saw some juicy grapes hanging high on a vine. He tried and tried to jump up and reach them, but no matter how hard he tried, he couldn't get to them. Finally, he gave up and walked away, saying, "Those grapes are probably sour anyway." He convinced himself he didn't want them, even though he did.
